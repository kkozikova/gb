THUS SANG THE TREES

Blender, Krita and DaVinci Resolve.

3d animation project made in the span of 2 and a half months. All visuals are created by me.

The aim of this project was to define a setting/ feel for the video game of the same title.

node workflows

Project uses scattering and procedural animations created through Blender's geonodes. Materials are created and adjusted in a similar manner.

rigging, animation, simulation

Two rigged characters (quadruped and biped) animated by hand. Both have procedural animation features such as- geoNodes displace animation and cloth simulation.

IREVERSE

Unity, Blender and After Effects

Solo endeavour. Created as a part of my masters thesis on control in video games.

Utilizes traditional as well as experimental animation and rigging techniques

mixed-media visuals

A multitude of techniques were utilized to create this title. Assets are made for the 2d world as well as the 3d world. The in-game news report was made in AE and Blender and exported as a video to the project.

Regarding CGI the in-game news report was the audience's favorite feature. It is made using a mix of Blender and After Effects, motion tracking and very customized facial rigging. You can find a quick explanation in the carousel above.

THUS SANG THE TREES

Unreal5, Blender, Figma, Maya, Krita and Substance Painter.

Developed by a group of master's students as a part of their thesis. Concepts and majority of the assets were created by me. The project utilizes mocap, procedrual and handmade animations.

Check out the game on itch.io!

models and optimisation

The models and textures are optimized for in-game use. Transparent textures have transparency maps and a lot of the texture files are re -used among the assets in the project.

ARIS ARCANUM

Unity, Blender and Krita

A lovecraftian roguelite developed by Broken Cane. My work was focused on the characters, their concepts and realisation.

Find more on the Official Page!

isometry and visibility

The concepts were developed in order to fit into the environment- to compliment the style and to be easily recognizeable for the isometric view. All of them are optimized for in-game usage.
